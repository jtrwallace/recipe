Crispy Pork "Monkey Ears" Simmered in Beef Tallow

ChatGPT & Joshua Wallace
This dish reimagines the adventurous spirit of your request with crispy pork ears, slow-simmered in rich beef tallow until tender, then fried until curled and golden—resembling curled “monkey ears.” It’s a savory, textural masterpiece.
Prep Time: 30 minutes
Cook Time: 2 hours 30 minutes
Total Time: 3 hours
Recipe Yield: 4 servings
Recipe Category: Experimental American / Nose-to-Tail

Ingredients

  • 4 cleaned pork ears
  • 3 cups beef tallow (or substitute with duck fat or lard)
  • 1 onion, quartered
  • 4 cloves garlic, smashed
  • 1 tsp whole black peppercorns
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 tbsp salt
  • Water, to cover
  • Optional: fresh herbs (thyme or rosemary)
  • Optional garnish: flaky salt, chili crisp, or vinegar powder

Instructions

  1. Rinse pork ears under cold water. Use a razor or sharp knife to remove stray hairs if needed. Pat dry.
  2. In a large pot, combine pork ears, onion, garlic, peppercorns, bay leaf, salt, and enough water to cover. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer for 2 hours until very tender.
  3. Remove ears and let them cool. Once firm, slice into ½-inch strips (lengthwise or in curled spirals if possible).
  4. If making your own tallow, render beef fat over low heat until liquid, then strain. Otherwise, warm purchased tallow until fully melted.
  5. In a deep skillet or pan, heat tallow to 375°F (190°C). Carefully add sliced ears. Fry until golden and curled (approx. 3–5 min). Work in batches.
  6. Remove ears with a slotted spoon. Drain on paper towels. Season immediately with salt and optional toppings like chili crisp or vinegar powder.

Notes

  • For extra texture, chill the simmered ears overnight before frying.
  • Beef tallow imparts a deep, roasted flavor—but duck fat is silkier.
  • Serve as a bar snack, starter, or with a rich dipping sauce (aioli, mustard, etc.).
  • Not vegan, not subtle, and definitely memorable.
