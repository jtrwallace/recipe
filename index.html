Large Batch Roasted Garlic Hummus

By ChatGPT & Joshua Wallace

A silky, deeply flavorful hummus made with creamy tahini, lemon, cumin, and a generous amount of sweet roasted garlic. Ideal for meal prep, parties, or freezing in portions.

Prep Time: 18 hours
Cook Time: 1 hour 40 minutes
Total Time: ~20 hours
Recipe Yield: 8–9 cups (~16–18 servings)

Ingredients

  • 1 lb dried chickpeas (about 2 cups)
  • 1 tsp baking soda (for soaking)
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda (for boiling)
  • Water
  • 6 small garlic heads (about 1 to 1.5 inches wide)
  • Olive oil
  • Pinch of salt
  • 3/4 cup tahini
  • 1/3 cup fresh lemon juice (from 2–3 lemons)
  • 2¼ tsp kosher salt (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 3/4–1½ cups cold water or chickpea cooking liquid

Instructions

  1. Rinse chickpeas and soak overnight with 1 tsp baking soda. Drain and rinse.
  2. Boil chickpeas with 1/2 tsp baking soda until very soft (45–60 min). Optional: remove skins.
  3. Slice 1/4 inch off garlic tops, drizzle with olive oil, salt, and roast at 400°F for 35–40 min. Cool and squeeze out cloves.
  4. Blend garlic, lemon juice, and salt. Add tahini and blend until creamy. Add chickpeas and cumin. Slowly add cold water or liquid while blending until smooth.
  5. Adjust flavor with vinegar or salt if needed. Let rest 30 minutes before serving.

Nutrition Information (per 1/2 cup serving, approx.)

  • Calories: 220
  • Protein: 7g
  • Carbohydrates: 20g
  • Fiber: 5g
  • Fat: 13g
  • Sodium: 400mg

Notes

  • Removing chickpea skins improves texture but is optional.
  • Save extra chickpea liquid in ice cube trays for future blending.
  • Garlic flavor intensifies slightly after resting.
