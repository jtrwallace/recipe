Bold and satisfying, these chorizo and egg burritos are loaded with savory spice from Mexican chorizo, sweetness from caramelized onions, and creamy avocado slices—all wrapped in a warm tortilla. Cheese is optional, perfect for customizing to your taste.
Author: ChatGPT & Joshua Wallace
Prep Time: 10 minutes
Cook Time: 15 minutes
Total Time: 25 minutes

Ingredients

Burrito Filling

  • 10 oz Mexican chorizo (raw, casing removed if needed)
  • 6 large eggs
  • 1 tablespoon milk or cream
  • 1 medium yellow onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil (or neutral oil)
  • Salt, to taste
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste

For Assembly

  • 4 large flour tortillas (10–12 inch)
  • 1 large ripe avocado, sliced
  • Optional: hot sauce or salsa of choice

Optional Toppings

  • Shredded cheddar or Monterey Jack cheese
  • Sour cream
  • Fresh cilantro leaves

Instructions

Cook the Onions

  1. In a large skillet over medium heat, add olive oil.
  2. Add sliced onions and a pinch of salt. Cook, stirring occasionally, until soft and golden brown—about 8–10 minutes. Remove from pan and set aside.

Cook the Chorizo

  1. In the same skillet, add chorizo and break it up with a wooden spoon.
  2. Cook until browned and cooked through, about 6–7 minutes. Drain excess fat if needed.

Make the Scrambled Eggs

  1. While chorizo cooks, whisk eggs with milk, salt, and pepper.
  2. Lower heat to medium-low. Pour eggs over the cooked chorizo. Stir gently and cook until softly scrambled and just set, about 2–3 minutes.
  3. Stir in cooked onions and turn off the heat.

Assemble the Burritos

  1. Warm tortillas in a dry skillet or microwave until pliable.
  2. Divide the chorizo-egg-onion mixture among the tortillas.
  3. Top each with sliced avocado.
  4. Add optional cheese, hot sauce, sour cream, or cilantro if desired.
  5. Fold the sides over the filling, then roll up tightly from the bottom into burritos.
Servings: 4 burritos

Notes

  1. Use soy chorizo for a vegetarian version—just cook separately and skip the eggs or replace them with tofu scramble.
  2. Add sautéed bell peppers or spinach for extra veggies.
  3. To freeze: wrap burritos in foil, then store in a freezer bag. Reheat in the oven or microwave when ready to eat.
